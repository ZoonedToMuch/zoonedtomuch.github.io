For junior schoolchildren of grades 1–2, the lesson is held on the topic “Why am I studying?”. They will be introduced to the public holiday - the Day of Knowledge, in order to create a favorable educational environment for the development of cognitive interest. The lessons of ejection of such values ​​as development, self-realization, friendship, as well as the normal development of a respectful attitude to knowledge, the desire to receive, learn a new, received positive mood for communication, for joint activities. The children will have to master knowledge in a person, they will give primary ideas about scientific and technological progress. The lesson will be held in the form of a heuristic conversation, designed for 30 minutes. Children will learn that earlier the school year in urban and rural schools began at different times. City children began to study in August, and rural children - in the fall, after the harvest, as the children helped their parents in this important matter. There was no single day for the start of the school year until 1935. Only in 1935, September 1 was established as a single date for the start of the academic year. Since 1984, our country has been celebrating a public holiday - the Day of Knowledge. Methodological materials offer video clips, presentation, game, group work with text for use. Pupils of grades 3-4 in the first lesson of the cycle will find out why a person needs knowledge. The lesson is aimed at developing interest in new knowledge, raising pride in one's country, forming an understanding of the importance of knowledge for achievements in science, culture, for the development of society and improving the quality of life. It is expected that the children will increase their desire to expand their erudition and general culture, and will show an emotional interest in knowledge and scientific discoveries. In the introductory part of the lesson, children will be presented for comparison with two pictures about life in a primitive society and a modern city - this will become a motive for discussing scientific achievements that have enriched the knowledge of mankind and opened the way for progress. They will talk with the guys about the most important discoveries throughout history - the taming of fire, the creation of the first nails, the first wheel, the first Russian steam locomotive, the subsequent discoveries of radio, air transport, and space exploration. As a result of the joint discussion, the students will make a conclusion about the value of human knowledge, write words for a poster on this topic in the process of group work. In grades 5-11, as well as for students of secondary vocational education, the lesson will be devoted to the topic “We are Russia. Opportunities are the future." It is expected that the lesson will give the children an idea of ​​the potential and benefits of self-realization and self-determination. They will be told about the platform “Russia – the Land of Opportunities”. Children will see a video greeting of the leaders and ambassadors of the projects of this platform. The lesson is also aimed at educating active patriotism as a value-semantic attitude to the Motherland, expressed in civic identity, social creation, bearing social responsibility through meaningful self-realization and self-determination. In addition, students will be introduced to the most significant events for them: the All-Russian Olympiad for schoolchildren and international Olympiads, they will motivate children to participate in them. For schoolchildren in grades 1–2, the lesson will be devoted to the topic “Where do we live?” The children will be taught to understand that native nature is part of the Motherland, and love for nature is a manifestation of love for one's Fatherland. The lesson is aimed at educating citizenship, love for one's country, pride in one's Fatherland. Children will be told how nature is described in poetry, visual arts, music and videos. They will complete tasks for imagination, fantasy, expressive reading of poetry. Schoolchildren will learn to participate in a dialogue, compare works of different types of art on the topic, purpose, expressive means; evaluate the mood that is born in the listener (spectator) when perceiving music, a literary word, a work of painting. They will work with interactive tasks: guess the sights, sounds and colors of nature. This will help develop auditory and visual perception, the ability to imagine pictures of nature filled with these sounds and colors. Consideration of an imaginary situation - "we are going to visit the Yakut hunter Tuigun" contributes to the development of fantasy, the imagination of schoolchildren.
